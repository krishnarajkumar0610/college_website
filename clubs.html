RED RIBBON CLUB

Dr. R. Aruna, Co-Ordinator

The Red Ribbon Club project was created by the New York-based Visual Aids Artists Caucus in 1991 with the motto of demonstrating compassion for people living with AIDS & their caregivers.

The Red Ribbon is a symbol for both drug abuse and the fight against AIDS. The Red Ribbon Foundation was created in 1993 with the objective of imparting education about the prevention of HIV and AIDS.

The Red Ribbon Club was established in our college during the year 2005.

Objectives

  • To imbibe a sense of humane values in students through yoga and meditation.
  • To spread awareness about HIV / AIDS among students.
  • To understand the problems of adolescence and their impact on performance.

Activities

  • AIDS awareness programme was organized on 14th March 2011. Mr. S. Palanichamy, RRC District Manager, Madurai, explained how AIDS can spread and its socio-attitudinal impact on students. More than 150 students participated in this programme.
  • Founder's Day Blood Donation Camp was organized on 29th June 2015 in the College Campus. RRC and NSS Volunteers actively participated in the camp.
