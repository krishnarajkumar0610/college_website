Thiagarajar Group of Institutions, Madurai, South India
Description:
The Foundation serves to identify, protect, enhance and popularise the traditional arts and crafts of Tamilnadu. It aims at identifying craftspersons in need of revival, support, promotion, and tapping new talent, especially among women and youth.
Target Groups:
This sector helps to develop 23 million artisans who are degraded by society because of their poverty and low social status.
Unemployed youth, college dropouts, relocated communities, families, and amateur craftspersons.
Training:
In the first stage, concentration and focus will be on the development of traditional crafts and crafts persons.
In the second stage, as a latent function, the foundation intends to give training to aspiring college students who have an interest in crafts work, as this could be a source of supplementary occupation and income.
